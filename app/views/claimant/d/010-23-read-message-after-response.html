Response

Dear ((Name))

You wrote to ask what steps you could take as you have a judgment against the defendant which they have not paid.
You can choose to enforce the judgement by asking the court to target different aspects of the defendant’s assets to get your money. The court can use:

  • a warrant of control - for goods owned by the defendant
  • an attachment of earnings order - for the defendant’s wages
  • a third-party debt order - for the defendant’s savings
  • a charging order - for the defendant’s assets

If you are owed £5,000 or more, you can also , apply to make the defendant bankrupt however, this can be expensive.

The court cannot guarantee you will get your money back, and you will have to pay a fee for any action you take. Although the court will add the fee to the money the defendant already owes, the court cannot return what you have paid if you do not get your money from the defendant, or if the court refuses your application.

To find out more about what do if you have a judgment but the defendant has not paid, visit: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/what-to-do-if-a-defendant-doesnt-pay-money-after-judgment-ex321/what-to-do-if-you-have-a-judgement-but-the-defendant-has-not-paid-ex321
