Understand what happens if the judgment is not paid

If the defendant has not paid the judgment, the claimant can try and get their money by asking the court to target different aspects of the defendant’s assets. This is called 'enforcing the judgment'. The court can use:

  • a warrant of control - for goods owned by the defendant
  • an attachment of earnings order - for the defendant’s wages
  • a third-party debt order - for the defendant’s savings
  • a charging order - for the defendant’s assets

Find more detail about what to do if a defendant does not pay money after a judgement (opens in a new window).

Fees

You will have to pay a fee when you ask the court to enforce the judgment, the amount depends on the method you select. Find out about civil court fees (opens in a new window).

Anything else

If you still need help on your claim, you can send a message to the court.

It takes at least 10 days for the court to respond to a message.
